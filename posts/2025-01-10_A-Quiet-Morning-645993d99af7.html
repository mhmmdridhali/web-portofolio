A Quiet Morning

Waking Up Together With You
Waking Up Together With You…

The morning does not speak,
 yet it hums your name in the folds of the sheets.
 I am an exile from sleep,
 wandering the map of your face — 
 the constellation of freckles,
 the arch of your brow,
 the horizon where your breath meets mine.

The sun trespasses through the blinds,
 its light weaving itself into your hair.
 It knows, as I do,
 that there is no poetry truer than this:
 waking up beside you,
 witnessing the quiet war of dawn and dreams.

The kettle hisses like a reluctant prophet,
 foretelling a day that will not know our secrets:
 how the world outside is loud and vast,
 but here, in the sanctuary of your arms,
 even silence feels crowded with meaning.

I do not need more than this.
 The world could end softly,
 like a song fading into static,
 and I would still find eternity
 in the echo of your yawn,
 the crumpled corners of your smile.

Love, you are not my escape.
 You are my return.
 A place where I wake, not just from sleep,
 but from the dreams that dared to forget
 the sound of your laughter
 pulling me gently back to life.

By Muhammad Ali Ridho on January 10, 2025.
