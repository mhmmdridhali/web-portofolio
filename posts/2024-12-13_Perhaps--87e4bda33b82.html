They asked, “why do you love her?”

The room feels smaller now. Or maybe it’s me who has shrunk. The walls don’t close in — they’ve simply stopped meaning anything without her here to fill the space. I sit by the window, her chair untouched across from me. The moonlight seeps through the glass, pale and cold, spilling over the empty table like a soft elegy.

“I thought I’d cry more,” I say, to no one and to her at once. My voice is steady, almost too calm.

“But the tears dried up somewhere along the way. What’s left now is… this silence.”

My eyes rest on her chair, the one where she always sat, her hands resting gently on her lap.

“Do you remember that train ride?” I ask, even though there’s no one here to answer.

“We swapped seats. I didn’t mind. You wanted the window and i wanted to look at you”

My words settle in the stillness, as if the room is listening. “It’s strange, isn’t it?” I continue, my tone even.

“How love doesn’t die, even when the person is gone. You’re not here, but the love… it stays, like it has nowhere else to go.”

The silence stretches, heavy but not unbearable. It’s just there, like the air around me. “I used to tell you, that if I ever saw a flaw in you, it would only mean my eyes were flawed. And now… all I see is this emptiness, and I wonder if that’s the flaw I never noticed.”

The moonlight shifts as the night goes on, painting the room in soft, pale light. “Because my love for you is higher than words,” I say, the words soft but clear, “I’ve fallen silent. But you’re still here. In the quiet. In my thoughts. In the words I’ll never say out loud again.”

A pause, as the universe listens.

“Until the stars burn out, and all worlds end.”

“Until the planets collide, and the suns wither.”

“Until the moon loses its light, and the seas dry up.”

“Until I grow so old that even your name slips from my memory.”

“Until my heart beats one last time, and my tongue can’t utter your name anymore.”

I close my eyes, letting out a slow breath.

“Only then… perhaps, I’ll stop loving you. Perhaps.”
