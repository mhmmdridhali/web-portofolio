Home Sweet Home

"Not For Me"
“Have You Ever Felt Homesick?”

I haven’t. Oddly enough, despite moving away from home, I’ve never felt an overwhelming desire to return.

Monotonous Life

My days passed much like they did back home — waking up, attending classes, grabbing meals, then returning to sleep. No tears, no strong urge to go back.

Monotone, stagnant, boring, some might say.

One thing that added a bit of color to my life was my mother’s daily texts. She ensured I didn’t feel forgotten, even if our conversations often revolved around meals and pray.

She seemed determined to make me feel safe, assuring me that no matter how bad my days got or the choices I made, she would always be there for me — if only through their prayers.

But as I realized that every visit home was initiated by my family, not me, I began to question: were they truly my home?

Because, surprisingly, I was okay even if I didn’t return. I felt safe, even if weeks passed without a single text or call.

At this point, I dared to say that homesickness was something I had never experienced.

What is The Meaning of “Home”

For me, homesick isn’t a longing for a place; it’s a longing for a feeling.

When I think about home, it’s not a specific location. Sometimes, even when I’m physically at home, that feeling of wanting to “go home” still lingers.

Home, instead, is a sensation. The relief of leaving an event you didn’t enjoy, the joy of spotting your mom across the street after a tough day at school, the exhilaration of an unexpected early dismissal, the odd comfort of being carried to bed by your dad after falling asleep on the couch, or the euphoria of finally lying down in your bed after a long trip.

It’s the smells, the sounds, the small moments — like sunlight filtering through trees on a familiar street — that remind you of childhood play.

In the end, home is that feeling of being done, the feeling of being on the right path, an inexplicable sense of safety that can’t be tied to a single place.

Perhaps that’s why I’ve realized that the home I seek can only be found in those fleeting moments of near-death, where life’s memories flash by in painful clarity. So, when I say I want to go home, maybe I’m subconsciously longing for the peace that comes with life’s end.

Or maybe, it’s because I’ve never truly left. I’ve always been here, locked inside this home called myself.
