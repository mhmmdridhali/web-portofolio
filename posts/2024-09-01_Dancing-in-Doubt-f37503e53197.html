Dancing in Doubt

"What if...?"
“What if I can’t do it? What if I fall further behind everyone else? What if my hands are never capable of creating anything?”

He felt it, something always close by, just out of reach. It followed him wherever he went, a shadow that wouldn’t leave him alone. He couldn’t see it, but he knew it was there, lurking.

"Who are you?" he wondered as he walked through the early morning streets, the world still quiet and asleep.

No answer. Just a heavy silence that made him feel small, insignificant.

The presence was close, like a shadow clinging to him, refusing to let go.

"Why are you here?" he whispered, looking at the empty chair across from him, feeling as though something—or someone—was sitting there, watching him.

There was no reply, but he could feel the answer deep in his bones. It was there to remind him, to keep him in line, to make sure he never forgot.

"What do you want from me?" he asked, almost begging now.

The silence was heavy, pressing down on him. It didn’t need to speak. Its presence was enough to fill his mind with doubts. Every time he tried to focus, to push forward, he started to question himself.

“What if I fail?”
“What if I’m not good enough?”
“What if I’m just wasting my time?”

The questions kept coming, digging into him, making him second-guess every move, every choice. The presence wrapped around him, tightening its hold, making him feel like he was sinking, drowning.

“What are you?” he asked again, his voice barely a whisper now, fear tightening his chest.

But deep down, he already knew. The presence was familiar, an old friend he never wanted to see again.

The fear of failure, the ever-present shadow that followed him wherever he went.It was always there, whispering in his ear, filling his head with doubts, making him question everything.

Failure has a way of creeping into every corner of life, silently following like a shadow that never leaves. For him, it wasn’t just an occasional worry; it was a constant presence, a weight that pressed down on him every moment of the day.

Imagine feeling trapped, as if invisible bars surrounded you, keeping you from moving freely.

That’s how he felt—like failure had imprisoned him, restricting his every move. Even during simple moments, like sitting down for a meal, the weight of failure sat across from him, making it impossible to find peace. When he tried to gather his courage and face the challenges of life, failure was there, mocking his efforts, making his hands tremble with doubt.

“Failure is terrifying, especially when all I want is to keep trying to be human.”
