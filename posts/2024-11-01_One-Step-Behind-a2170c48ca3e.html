One Step Behind

"The World’s Running a Marathon, and I’m Still Tying My Shoes"
“Have You Ever Felt a Step Behind?”

Have you ever felt like the world is moving too fast, and you're just a step behind? I know I have. I step outside, and it feels like the entire world is in fast-forward—cars zipping by, people hustling from one moment to the next. There’s this heavy pressure in the air, like everyone’s caught in an invisible race, always striving to be somewhere or someone, yet the destination seems to drift further away the closer we get.

I try to match the pace, but there's always that lingering feeling, like the world is playing a game of musical chairs, and I'm the one left standing when the music stops.

Everyday Moments

Even in the quiet moments, it’s hard to escape this pressure—the phone buzzing with notifications, unread messages, a growing list of expectations from every direction. It feels like swimming against an endless current, each stroke harder than the last.

My to-do list grows faster than I can cross things off, and in the blur of constant movement, there’s barely a second to breathe. Sometimes, I think about what it would be like to just pause everything for a single day, to let the chaos settle, so maybe I could see life a bit more clearly.

“Sometimes the most productive you can do is to relax.”

There is a day when I find myself in the middle of a crowd, surrounded by people, yet feeling completely alone. Not knowing what to do. Not knowing where to go. Not knowing who to talk to. Not knowing who I am.

The pace of the crowd gets faster and faster, but my heart seems to slow down. Although it’s noisy out there, I just can’t hear anything. Each minute stretches into eternity. Everyone around me seems to have a goal, moving with purpose, while I’m just standing in the same old spot.

And today, I see a kid on a bicycle, pedaling with nothing but pure joy, without a thought about where they need to be or what time it is. Watching that, I remember a time when life felt like that for me too—when I wasn’t tied to the clock, when freedom wasn’t something I had to plan.

Somewhere along the line, we traded that thrill for efficiency, swapped wonder for schedules, and I can’t help but wonder if it was worth the trade.

Seeking Balance in My Own Rhythm

People always talk about finding balance, as if there's this magical place where life doesn’t feel like a race. But it’s tricky when everything around me tilts to one side, pushing me forward without a pause.

Maybe it’s not about finding time but choosing it.

Maybe it means learning to slow down, even if the world keeps spinning at full speed.

Maybe it’s okay to say "no" sometimes, not because I'm too tired, but because I deserve to experience life with open eyes and a light heart.

In the end, it’s not about being the fastest or doing the most; it’s about finding my own rhythm in a world that’s always sprinting.

And maybe, just maybe, being a step behind isn’t a failure — it’s just the space I need—to breathe, to see, to live.
